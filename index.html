Here a Simple intro into Simple Present and Present Continuous Tense

Simple Present Tense

We use the present simple tense when we want to talk about fixed habits or routines – things that don’t change.

Present Continuous Tense

We use the present continuous to talk about actions which are happening at the present moment, but will soon finish.

Simple Present Tense

Subject + Verb (s/es) (Positive)

  • I/You/They/We + Verb

  • She/He/It + Verb (s/es)

Subject + Do/Does not + Verb (Negative)

  • I/You/They/We + Do not + Verb

  • She/He/It + Does not + Verb (s/es)

Do/Does + Subject + Verb? (Introgative)

  • Do + I/You/They/We + Verb + ?

  • Does + She/He/It + Verb + ?

Exceptions when adding 's' :

  • For can, may, might, must, do not add s.

    • Example: he can, she may, it must

  • After o, ch, sh or s, add es.

    • Example: do - he does, wash - she washes

  • After a consonant, the final consonant y becomes ie. (but: not after a vowel)

    • Example: worry - he worries

    but: play - he plays

Present Continuous Tense

Subject + To be (am/is/are) + Verb (ing)

  • I + Am + Verb (ing) (Positive)

  • You/They/We + Are + Verb (ing)

  • She/He/It + Is+ Verb (ing)

Subject + To be (am/is/are) + Not + Verb (ing) (Negative)

  • I + Am not + Verb (ing)

  • You/They/We + Are + Not + Verb (ing)

  • She/He/It + Is + Not + Verb (ing)

To be (am/is/are) + Subject + Verb (ing) + ? (Introgative)

  • Am + I + Verb (ing) + ?

  • Are + You/They/We + Verb (ing) + ?

  • Is + She/He/It + Verb (ing) + ?

Exceptions when adding 'ing'

  • Silent e is dropped. (but: does not apply for -ee)

    • Example: come - coming but: agree - agreeing

  • After a short, stressed vowel, the final consonant is

    • Example: sit – sitting

  • After a vowel, the final consonant l is doubled in British English (but not in American English).

    • Example: travel - travelling (British English) but: traveling (American English)

  • Final ie becomes y.

    • Example: lie - lying
